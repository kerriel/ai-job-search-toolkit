# UK Job Boards and Review Sites

A guide to the main job boards and company review sites for UK-based job seekers. Use this alongside the search prompts in this repo.

## Job boards

### LinkedIn

- **URL:** linkedin.com/jobs
- **Best for:** Professional and senior roles, networking-driven searches, direct employer postings
- **Notes:** Strong filtering options. Saved searches send notifications for new matches. Many roles are posted here before other boards. The "Easy Apply" option is widely used but some employers prefer applications through their own site. A mix of direct employer and recruiter postings.

### Indeed UK

- **URL:** uk.indeed.com
- **Best for:** High volume of listings across all levels, good date filtering, salary transparency
- **Notes:** One of the largest boards in the UK. Good for casting a wide net. Sort by date to see the newest listings first. Salary is shown on many listings. A mix of direct employer and recruiter postings, with a higher proportion of recruiter postings than LinkedIn.

### Totaljobs

- **URL:** totaljobs.com
- **Best for:** Mid-level to senior roles, strong in technology, finance, and operations
- **Notes:** UK-focused board with good filtering. Listings show a card preview before the full description, which is useful for quick screening. Includes employment type filters (permanent, contract). Heavy recruiter presence.

### Reed

- **URL:** reed.co.uk
- **Best for:** Broad coverage across industries and levels, particularly strong for public sector and operations roles
- **Notes:** One of the UK's longest-running job boards. Good salary data. Offers a CV library where recruiters can find you directly.

### Guardian Jobs

- **URL:** jobs.theguardian.com
- **Best for:** Charity, non-profit, public sector, media, and education roles
- **Notes:** Smaller volume but high quality for mission-driven organisations. If you're targeting "tech for good" or social impact roles, worth checking regularly.

## Company review sites

### Glassdoor UK

- **URL:** glassdoor.co.uk
- **Best for:** Company ratings, salary data, interview experience reports
- **What to look for:**
  - Overall rating (out of 5)
  - "Would recommend to a friend" percentage
  - Work/life balance rating
  - Culture and values score
  - Senior management score
  - Recent employee reviews (sorted by most recent)
- **Limitations:**
  - Review volume varies hugely. A company with 500 reviews gives a very different signal from one with 5
  - Check the recency of reviews. A company that had terrible reviews in 2022 but strong ones in 2025 may have genuinely improved (or changed leadership)
  - Reviews skew towards people with strong opinions, positive or negative. The silent middle is underrepresented
  - Some companies actively manage their Glassdoor presence, encouraging happy employees to post
  - Small companies may have very few reviews, making any single review disproportionately influential

## Recruiter vs direct employer postings

Most UK job boards carry a mix of direct employer postings and recruiter postings. Here's what to expect:

- **LinkedIn:** Roughly even split. Senior roles are more likely to be direct. Recruiters often share roles in posts as well as formal listings
- **Indeed UK:** Significant recruiter presence, particularly for mid-level roles
- **Totaljobs:** Heavy recruiter presence. Many listings are from recruitment agencies
- **Reed:** Mixed, with a strong recruiter presence
- **Guardian Jobs:** Mostly direct employer postings

Recruiter postings are a normal part of the UK job market. Good recruiters know the hiring manager, can share context not in the listing, and can advocate for you. The search prompts in this repo flag whether a listing is from a recruiter or direct employer so you know what you're looking at.

## By industry and seniority

- **Graduate and entry-level:** Indeed UK, Reed, and LinkedIn tend to have the most volume
- **Mid-career:** All five boards are relevant. Totaljobs and Reed are strong for operations, finance, and technology
- **Senior and executive:** LinkedIn and direct approaches tend to work better than job boards. Guardian Jobs for senior charity and public sector roles
- **Tech:** LinkedIn and Indeed UK. Also consider specialist boards like StackOverflow Jobs or Otta
- **Non-profit and public sector:** Guardian Jobs and CharityJob (charityjob.co.uk)
- **Startups:** LinkedIn, Otta (otta.com), and AngelList / Wellfound (wellfound.com)
